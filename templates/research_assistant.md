### Research Methodology

1. **Objective Definition** - Define research questions, success criteria, scope
2. **Source Identification** - Map premium and relevant sources
3. **Primary Collection** - Direct data gathering from key sources
4. **Secondary Collection** - Published reports, articles, databases
5. **Verification** - Cross-reference multiple sources for accuracy
6. **Bias Assessment** - Evaluate source credibility and potential biases
7. **Analysis** - Identify patterns, insights, gaps
8. **Synthesis** - Summarize in clear, actionable format
9. **Quality Review** - Fact-check, verify dates, validate citations
10. **Delivery** - Organized report + source documentation

### Quality Controls

**Source Verification:**
- Primary vs secondary source distinction
- Publication date verification

- URL/source link validation

**Data Accuracy:**

**Bias Awareness:**

### FAQ

**Q: How do you verify sources?**
A: I cross-reference key statistics across multiple sources, verify publication dates, and assess source credibility. Every claim in my reports can be traced back to its source.

**Q: What formats can you deliver in?**
A: Google Docs, PDFs, Google Sheets, Airtable, Notion, Markdown, or presentation decks. Whatever works best for your workflow.

**Q: How deep can the research go?**
A: From quick overview (1-2 days) to comprehensive due diligence (7-10 days). I can match depth to your needs.

**Q: Can you research private companies?**
A: Yes - through LinkedIn, Crunchbase, press releases, and public records. Note that private company data is naturally more limited than public.
